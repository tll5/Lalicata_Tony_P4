Brief History of Some Key Destinations Around the World




Mexico City

Now the sixth largest city in the world and capital of Mexico, Mexico City sits atop a high valley and has a long history that dates back to the Indigenous peoples of the Aztec Tempire. Originally, this entire city was once a giant lake known as Lake Texcoco, which would later hold an island city known as Tenochtitlan, one of the thriving cities of the Aztec peoples throughout 14th and 15th century. Post-European colonization of the island that was once Tenochtitlan, settlers drained the lake water over time for resources, allowing them to expand the land in the high valley and eventually grow into one of the largest and most populous cities in the world. Cairo

Cairo is the capital of Egypt, a country that sits at the top of the list in terms of historical significance. This city and its surrounding area sit along the Nile River and it is home to many different diverse cultures from Africa as well as parts of the Middle East. It is one of the largest and oldest cities in Africa and close in proximity to The Great Sphinx and the Pyramids of Giza, which were built by the Ancient Egyptians over 4,500 years ago.
