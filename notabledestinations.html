Five Destinations to Visit on your Next Trip!




5. Japan

Fun Fact: 70% of Japan consists of forests, mountains, and volcanoes, but is also home to the world's most populated city, Tokyo!

Japan is home to over 125 million people, and approximately one out of every four people are likely a resident of Tokyo. Despite having little natural resources, Japan has managed to become one of the world's largest and strongest economies. Between its beautiful, mountains terrain contrasted by its massive cities full of history, war, art, and music, Japan is one of most intriguing hubs for any travelers wanting to visit. 4. Brazil

Fun Fact: Most larger cities in Brazil lie on the southeastern coast due to over 60% of the Amazon Rain Forest being inside the country, which shows how massive it is since Brazil is the world's fifth largest country by size.

Brazil, while large in size and population, also has dense rainforest along with beautiful, numerous beaches along its eastern coast. Brazil is largely known for soccer, unique food, culture, and festivals! Brazil is home to many famous music festivals that occur year-round. One festival in particular is Carnival, which involves large-scale street dancing, gatherings, lights, and parades that go on through entire nights into the next day. Brazil also has many religious and historical attractions as well, such as the Christ the Redeemer statue which alone attracts thousands of tourists daily. 3. Ireland

Fun Fact: The holiday of Halloween (although much different than we know it today) originated as a traditional festival in Ireland!

Ireland for centuries has been widely known to be a beautiful, green island country. However, the main island of Ireland consists of Ireland and Northern Ireland. The capital of Ireland is Dublin, and the capital of Northern Ireland is Belfast. Although they have differences, what they largely share are their beautiful towns, food and drinks, numerous hills and cliffs, festivals, rich history, and much more. Italy has it all: history even dated prior to the Roman Empire, diverse culture, art amazing food, churches, cities, and several major tourist attractions, including the leaning Tower of Pisa and the Colosseum. Whether you are visiting for the history in Italy's capital, Rome, the unmatched beauty of Venice, or the calm scenery of Naples, the entire country is a top travel destination in every aspect. 1. New Zealand

Fun Fact: There are more than 5 sheep for every person living in New Zealand!

There is one major reason that I would recommend travelers visit New Zealand over any other country in the world: its near untouched wilderness and landscape. Although New Zealand is approximately the same size as England, it is home to only 5 million people. The country has very few major cities, with its capital, Auckland, being the most dense and populous, but still very small in comparison to other major cities around the world. The country is known for being the setting for countless action and fantasy movies that use the country's varied and pristine landscape. For instance, New Zealand native Peter Jackson filmed the entire "Lord of the Rings" movie trilogy with minimal CGI and the help of the beautiful land of New Zealand. In essence, I would tell any individual looking to travel internationally to visit New Zealand first, and it is a country I have yet to visit, but one I am determined to see at least once in my life.
